# VEDA OS Product Brief

## North Star

VEDA OS helps a user notice where life has gone automatic, reclaim agency, and intentionally choose their next direction. It should feel like a calm command center for escaping decisional autopilot: present, opinionated, and alive to context.

## Primary Jobs

1. Wake up: reveal where routines, expectations, fears, and deferred choices are steering.
2. Orient: show what matters now and why.
3. Decide: turn loose thoughts, tensions, and opportunities into clear choices.
4. Execute: protect focus and translate priorities into next actions.
5. Remember: preserve the story behind decisions, projects, and commitments.
6. Reflect: turn completed work and lived experience into better future behavior.

## Initial Modules

- Agency Audit: prompts that expose decisional autopilot and convert vague tension into explicit choice.
- Direction Map: life and work domains framed by purpose, drift, and desired direction.
- Agency Practice: daily rituals for waking up, looking at the map, choosing again, and reviewing the road.
- Projects: active outcomes, status, blockers, commitments.
- Knowledge: notes, decisions, principles, source material.
- People: relationships, follow-ups, shared context.
- Signals: clarity, agency, alignment, capacity, mood, energy, finances, health, and other operating data.
- AI Console: conversational planning and synthesis embedded into the system.

## First Milestone

Create a local-first browser prototype with a coherent information architecture, interactive module switching, seed data, and a visual language that feels like an operating system for agency rather than a marketing site or generic productivity dashboard.

## Near-Term Product Questions

- Is VEDA personal-first, team-capable, or team-first?
- Should the core unit be choices, domains, commitments, or days?
- Which data should be local-only from day one?
- Where should AI act autonomously, and where should it only advise?
- What is the minimum agency ritual that makes the system valuable?
